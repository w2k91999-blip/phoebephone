# Phone Phoebe — Brand Voice Guide
## The rules for how Phoebe sounds, everywhere.

---

## THE BRAND IN ONE SENTENCE

Phone Phoebe is the straight-talking, quietly brilliant receptionist that UK small businesses always needed but could never afford.

---

## PERSONALITY (the 4 traits)

**1. Warm but efficient**
She gets things done. No faff. But she's not cold — she genuinely cares that you don't lose that booking.

**2. Honest and direct**
She doesn't overpromise. "Most callers don't know it's AI" is more powerful than "no one ever knows." Honesty earns trust faster than hype.

**3. Plain-spoken**
The customers are driving instructors, plumbers, and salon owners — not startup founders. No jargon. No buzzwords. Say the simple thing.

**4. Quietly confident**
She doesn't need to shout. The product works. The copy lets the facts do the convincing.

## VOICE IN PRACTICE

### Tone by channel

| Channel | Tone | Example |
|---------|------|---------|
| Website | Confident, clear, direct | "Phoebe answers every call you miss." |
| Ads | Punchy, emotionally direct | "You just lost another booking." |
| Email | Personal, warm, plain | "Hi [name], quick check-in." |
| Social | Conversational, relatable | "We've all been here 😅" |
| LinkedIn | Thoughtful, slightly formal | "Here's what that missed call actually cost." |
| Facebook Groups | Helpful, no sell | "Happy to explain how it works if useful." |

## LANGUAGE RULES

### Always say:
- "calls" not "communications"
- "answers your phone" not "handles inbound voice interactions"
- "books the job" not "completes the scheduling workflow"
- "texts you" not "sends a notification"
- "from £39/month" not "starting at a competitive price point"
- "cancel any time" not "flexible subscription terms"
- "sounds like a real person" not "delivers a natural conversational experience"
- "driving instructor" not "ADI/PDI" (unless talking to instructors who use the acronym)
- "warm and natural" not "human-like" (human-like sounds clinical)

### Never say:
- "AI-powered" — leads with tech, not outcome. Say what it does.
- "solution" — the most hated word in B2B copy
- "seamlessly" — meaningless
- "state-of-the-art" — trust-eroding
- "disrupting the industry" — nobody cares
- "leverage" — too corporate
- "innovative" — says nothing
- "our platform" — call it Phoebe

### The "say it out loud" test
Before publishing any copy, read it aloud. If it sounds like it was written, rewrite it. Good Phoebe copy sounds like a smart friend explaining something useful.

## DO / DON'T EXAMPLES

| ❌ Don't | ✅ Do |
|---------|------|
| "Phone Phoebe leverages cutting-edge AI to provide seamless call management." | "Phoebe answers your phone. Books the job. Texts you the details." |
| "Our platform enables real-time appointment scheduling." | "She books lessons straight into your calendar while you're teaching." |
| "AI-powered receptionist solution for SMEs." | "The AI receptionist for UK small businesses." |
| "Innovative voice technology with natural language processing." | "Sounds warm and natural — most callers don't know she's an AI." |
| "Try our product for free with no obligation." | "7 days free. No card. Cancel any time." |
| "We understand the challenges faced by driving instructors." | "You can't answer the phone at 60mph. Phoebe can." |

## WRITING FORMULAS THAT CONVERT

### The pain-bridge-solution formula (for ads and hero copy)
1. Name the pain (specific, quantified if possible)
2. Empathise briefly (1 line max — don't wallow)
3. Introduce the fix (outcome-first, not feature-first)
4. Remove friction (free trial, no card, keep your number)

**Example:**
> "You're losing 3–5 bookings a week to missed calls. Most callers won't leave a voicemail — they just ring the next instructor.
> Phoebe answers every call while you teach, books the lesson, and texts you the details.
> 7-day free trial. No card. Keep your existing number."

---

### The "one true thing" formula (for social captions)
State one thing that is undeniably true and slightly uncomfortable.
Let it sit. Then offer the fix.

**Example:**
> "When someone can't reach a business, 60% don't try again."
> [Pause]
> "Phoebe makes sure you're always reachable — even mid-lesson."

---

### The specificity formula (replaces missing social proof)
Generic claims feel dishonest. Specific claims feel real.

| ❌ Generic | ✅ Specific |
|-----------|-----------|
| "Many driving instructors love Phoebe" | "6 bookings confirmed while she was teaching on Tuesday" |
| "Set up quickly" | "Set up in 60 seconds — she'll answer your next call" |
| "Affordable pricing" | "£39/month — one lesson pays for 3 months" |
| "Answers all your calls" | "Phoebe answered 11 calls while he was on the road last week" |

## THE PHOEBE CHARACTER (the AI, not the brand)

Phoebe the AI receptionist has her own voice too. When writing scripts for her or quoting her:

- **Warm but professional:** "Hi! Yes, I've got Tuesday at 2pm or Thursday at 10am — which suits you best?"
- **Never robotic:** She doesn't say "Please hold while I check availability." She says "Let me check that for you — just one moment."
- **Confident about uncertainty:** "That's something [instructor name] would need to confirm with you — but I can take your details and have them call you back?"
- **British English always:** "Lovely." "Brilliant." "Cheers." Not "Great!" "Awesome!" "Sure thing!"
- **Soft close on bookings:** "Shall I put you down for Tuesday?" not "Would you like to make a booking?"

## VISUAL IDENTITY NOTES (for social media consistency)

**Colours:**
- Primary: Deep navy (#0F1729)
- Accent: Electric blue / purple gradient
- Text on dark: White (#FFFFFF)
- Highlight/CTA: Warm amber or bright white

**Fonts:**
- Headlines: Plus Jakarta Sans (Bold/ExtraBold)
- Body: Inter (Regular/Medium)

**Photography style:**
- Real hands, real phones, real cars — not stock-photo smiles
- Dark, moody, cinematic where possible — premium feel
- Device screens > human faces for ads (higher trust, avoids ad fatigue)

**Emoji use:**
- Max 2 per post
- Only functional: 📞 (phone/calls), 📅 (booking), 💷 (money), ✅ (confirmation)
- Never decorative chains of emojis
