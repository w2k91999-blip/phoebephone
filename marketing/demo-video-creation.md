# Creating an AI Demo Call Video for Phone Phoebe

You need a short video (15–60 seconds) of a realistic phone call with Phoebe.
Here are 3 routes from easiest to most polished.

---

## OPTION 1 — Record a Real Phoebe Call (Easiest, Most Authentic)

Once your Vapi agent is provisioned:

1. Call your Phoebe number from a different phone
2. Play the role of a customer: "Hi, do you have any driving lessons free this week?"
3. Let Phoebe answer naturally and book you in
4. In the Vapi dashboard (dashboard.vapi.ai → Calls), download the call recording
5. Trim it to 30–45 seconds using any video editor (iMovie, CapCut, even Photos on Mac)
6. Add captions (CapCut does this automatically — paste in the transcript from Vapi)

**This is the most credible content you can post.** A real call, real voice, real booking.

---

## OPTION 2 — AI-Generated Demo Audio (No Live System Needed)

Use **ElevenLabs** to generate both sides of the conversation as separate audio tracks, then combine them.

### Step 1 — Generate the voices
Go to elevenlabs.io → Text to Speech

**Customer voice** (use a different voice — something natural, male or female):
> "Hi, do you have any driving lessons free this week?"
> [pause]
> "Tuesday's perfect."
> [pause]
> "Sam, 07700 900123."

**Phoebe's voice** (use the same ElevenLabs voice you picked for the actual agent):
> "Hi! Yes — I've got Tuesday at 2pm or Thursday at 10am. Which suits you best?"
> [pause]
> "Lovely. Can I take your name and number to confirm the booking?"
> [pause]
> "Thanks Sam — you're booked in for Tuesday at 2pm. You'll get a text confirmation shortly!"

Download both as MP3 files.

### Step 2 — Stitch the audio together
Use **GarageBand** (free on Mac):
- Import both tracks
- Interleave the lines so it sounds like a natural conversation with realistic gaps
- Export as M4A or MP3

### Step 3 — Make it visual
**Option A — CapCut (free, easy, mobile or desktop):**
- Use a phone frame template
- Add the audio
- Auto-generate captions
- Export as 9:16 for Reels/TikTok or 16:9 for YouTube/LinkedIn

**Option B — Canva (free):**
- Use a phone mockup from their templates
- Add the audio as background
- Animate captions to appear in time with speech
